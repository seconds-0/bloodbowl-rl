**D123-A — R6 spec corrected (v1: dual dodge/GFI counters) + R6v2 reframed around per-screener "fragility" to tame the combinatorial-displacement problem (Alex, 2026-06-15).** Both edits landed directly in `docs/reward-design-coaching-wisdom.md` (the R6/R6-refinement sections), this entry records the WHY.

**v1 correction (folded into R6's table row + sketch, not a new version)**: the original BFS labeled GFI-only squares as "free" (non-dodge), so a path needing 0 dodges but 1+ GFIs scored `min_dodges==0` -- the SAME full -0.05 as a true free walk-up. This is backwards from Alex's stated minimum bar: *"make them dodge to get to you, OR make them GFI to get to you -- do those things at a minimum."* Fix: BFS now tracks `(min_dodges, min_gfis)` as two counters; "free walk-up" (full -0.05) requires BOTH zero. One-line change to edge-cost bookkeeping, but load-bearing -- without it v1 doesn't actually implement what was asked.

**v2 reframed**: Alex correctly identified that the original v2 sketch (Grab lets the attacker choose the push square) is only ONE of FOUR ways a screener stops being an obstacle: (a) even non-Grab pushes have up to 3 attacker-chosen destination squares; (b) Blitz changes the attack angle, rotating which 3 squares those are; (c) Frenzy forces recursive follow-up blocks, each with its own (a)+(b) branching; (d) a no-push knockdown (Both Down/Defender Down) leaves the screener prone IN PLACE, removing its TZ without any push geometry. The cross-product is exponential -- "a really big exploding combinatorial problem" as stated.

THE REFRAME (avoids enumeration entirely): compute, once per screener, a single scalar `fragility = P(this piece stops projecting a TZ on the relevant square this turn, via knockdown-in-place OR being pushed off, vs its single best CURRENT adjacent threat)` -- reusing the existing per-pair `bb_blockev` math (same inputs as k_kd/k_value/k_ball, no new EV engine). Feed `fragility` into the BFS as a CONTINUOUS edge-cost (fragile screener ~ free square; solid screener ~ full obstacle) rather than enumerating where pieces end up. This is "is this obstacle solid" as ONE NUMBER from EXISTING machinery.

SPLIT: **v2a** (shippable) = fragility restricted to current adjacency, covers (a)+(d) -- already catches Alex's literal Halfling/Chaos-Warrior example (Warrior is already adjacent, no repositioning needed to matter). **v2b** (research, not scoped) = folding in (b) Blitz-angle + (c) Frenzy-chains -- if ever needed, via SAMPLING (Monte Carlo rollouts a la `tools/blockev_mc.c`), not exact enumeration, and only if v2a's canary proves insufficient (a SECOND, more specific trip-wire than v2's general one).

**D133 — BACKLOG (Alex design, 2026-06-16): R12 = defensive scoring-lane threat — the DUAL of R6v1 carrier-exposure.** Alex flagged a real asymmetry: R6v1 prices only OUR ball protection (when WE hold the ball, is the carrier reachable without a dodge/GFI?). There is NO corresponding DEFENSIVE term — when the OPPONENT holds the ball, have we left a free lane to our endzone? Nothing currently prices defensive positioning at all (why untrained defenders clump/ball-chase instead of screening). The symmetry is sound: if pricing "is my carrier exposed" drives offense, pricing "can the enemy score for free" should drive sane defense.

**v1 (Alex's stated minimum = one turn):** reuse R6v1's `bb_reach_field_compute` (already a `(dodges,gfis)` Dijkstra from any mover to every square) as the DUAL — mover = enemy ball carrier (fires only when opponent holds the ball, mirroring R6v1's gate), target = our defensive endzone LINE (`x = bb_endzone_x(enemy_team)`), classify min `(dodges,gfis)` cost over that line within carrier `MA + bb_max_rushes`: `(0,0)`→FREE WALK-IN (defense breached), total==1→soft (one dodge OR GFI to score), ≥2/unreachable→screened. Fire at MY-turn-end when the opponent holds the ball (symmetric to R6v1's own-turn-end). Connection: R6v1's endzone-EXEMPTION already does a CRUDE version (Chebyshev distance ≤ MA+rushes, but IGNORES tackle zones) — R12 is that upgraded to real screen-aware reachability.

**Why potentially HIGHER value than R6v1:** offense exposure = one opponent reaching one carrier; defense = carrier reaching a whole LINE, so a SINGLE hole anywhere breaches the screen. "Cover every lane" is strictly harder than "protect one piece" and is exactly where RL defenders fail — a free-lane penalty would directly teach screen/deep-safety discipline.

**REFINED STAGING (Alex, 2026-06-16) — the v2 split is the key insight: carrier-only stays POLYNOMIAL, only v3 explodes:**
- **v1** — binary free-lane (0-dodge/0-GFI for the current carrier this turn). One Dijkstra.
- **v1.5** — graded by the NUMBER of dodges/GFIs required (continuous threat probability vs binary). Same engine, richer cost readout. Mirrors R6v1.5's graduated-probability refinement → keeps offense/defense symmetric.
- **v2** — two-turn, **CARRIER ONLY** (no passing, no handoffs): can THIS carrier reach the endzone in two of its own activations? Still SINGLE-PIECE pathfinding, just a larger budget (≈ 2×(MA+rushes), counting dodges/GFIs en route) — polynomial, NO explosion, because the ball never changes hands so no new movers enter. Catches the most common real defensive failure ("you left a two-move-deep hole"). Caveat to make explicit: v2 implicitly assumes the defense does NOT re-screen between the opponent's two turns (in real BB you'd get an intervening turn to plug it) → v2 slightly OVER-estimates threat, but that's the SAFE direction for a shaping signal (teaches "don't even leave a two-move lane"); modeling the re-screen turns it into a game-tree, defeating the point. Canary, not exact-solve.
- **v3** — the EXPONENTIAL case: any handoff or pass. THIS is what blows up — the ball can transfer to a fresh piece (new mover, own MA) or teleport via a throw, so it's no longer single-piece pathfinding but a search over which teammate gets the ball and from where. Defer; if ever built, via SAMPLING (à la tools/blockev_mc.c), only if v2's canary proves insufficient.

**D133-A — R12 REFRAMED (Alex, 2026-06-16): measure PLAYERS not the BALL — dissolves the v3 exponential entirely; the simpler model is also MORE defensively correct.** Alex's key insight: humans don't compute the ball's exponential branching to assess scoring threat — they look at which OPPOSING PLAYERS can get downfield. Shift the question from "where can the ball go?" (branches through handoffs/passes = the v3 blowup) to "who can reach my endzone by movement?" (per-player, O(players)). LEGITIMATE because ball-delivery is cheap and player movement is the binding constraint — a fast deep player is a scoring threat BEFORE it holds the ball (the ball can be handed/thrown to it). Measuring players instead of the ball DELETES the need to model ball-transfer, which was the entire source of the v3 explosion.

**THE METRIC (replaces the carrier-reachability v1):** per opposing player `p`: `reach_per_turn(p) = MA(p) + max_GFIs(p)` (≈+2, +3 w/ Sprint); `turns_to_score(p) = ceil(dist_to_our_endzone(p) / reach_per_turn(p))`; count `n_threats_1turn`(≤1) and `n_threats_2turn`(≤2). Two turns = "same thing, ×2 budget" — O(players), one subtract + divide each, NO Dijkstra, NO ball model. **Mitigation = the defensive job:** a MARKED deep player has reduced effective reach (must dodge to leave, costing tempo + turnover risk), so the live signal = **unmitigated deep threats** = count players who can score in ≤N turns AND are unmarked. Minimizing that = "mark/cover the deepest movers" = the human heuristic; policy learns to body the fast deep piece instead of clumping on the ball.

**WHY MORE CORRECT THAN THE CARRIER-CENTRIC VERSION:** the defensive failure vs fast teams isn't the carrier strolling through your screen — it's an UNCOVERED DEEP RECEIVER your carrier-lane screen never accounted for. Player-centric "how many pieces can get deep" is the right object. HONEST TRADE: drop tackle-zone-aware PATH precision (raw distance vs screened path) for ball-agnosticism + O(players) cost — but for deep coverage that's correct, because a screen between carrier and endzone does NOTHING to cover an open downfield receiver, so path-cost was measuring the wrong thing. Marking, not lane-pathing, is how you address a deep mover.

**SUPERSEDES the reachability-dual as R12 v1.** Revised ladder: v1 = unmitigated deep-threat count (1-turn); v1.5 = graded (margin = reach − distance, and/or degree-of-marking); v2 = 2-turn (just ×2 reach budget); v3 (handoff/pass) DISSOLVED — never needed. Also a natural **obs FEATURE, not just reward** — "this enemy is a 1-turn scoring threat" is exactly the state a defender needs (per-player threat flag in the entity rows). The reachability-dual framing (D133 above) stays as the OPTIONAL lane-denial complement (cover the carrier's walk-in path) but is secondary to deep-mover coverage.
