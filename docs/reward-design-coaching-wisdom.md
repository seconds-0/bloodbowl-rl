# Reward-Shaping Memo: Coaching-Doctrine Knobs for BB2025 PPO Self-Play

**Scope.** Additions to the existing vector (win +3 / loss −3 / draw −0.5; TD ±1; possession +0.1/−0.5 settled-transitions; injury ±0.15 KO+CAS; setup ±0.25). All magnitudes relative to TD = 1.0. Six research angles deduplicated: the two turn-ordering candidates are merged (R1), the uphill-block penalty is folded into the p-scaled block surcharge (R2), and the two carrier-protection candidates are merged (R3). Engine hooks verified in-repo: `BB_ACT_SECURE_BALL` at `engine/include/bb/bb_actions.h:75`, Secure-the-Ball logic in `engine/src/proc_move.c`, send-off/bribe flow in `engine/data/spec/inducements.yaml`, settled-transition machinery already used by the possession knob.

**Design invariants applied throughout** (from the researchers' cross-cutting notes):

- Penalty-only wherever possible. Penalties can be over-avoided but not farmed; symmetric positives in self-play are farmable by both agents colluding on the trigger.
- Charge the **decision** (declaration/attempt or settled end-state), never the roll outcome — outcome-conditioned shaping rewards luck and teaches superstition.
- Every knob at least an order of magnitude below the reward of the behavior it gates; combined per-turn positional budget capped at ~−0.3 (< 1/3 TD).
- Engine-exact predicates only (stance, TZ map, activation flags, dice context, BFS reachability) — never position names, formation shapes, or threshold constants doctrine doesn't supply.
- Universal exemptions the guides themselves teach: final activation of the team turn, and turn 8/16 of a half ("nothing left to lose"). Key on "team has no further turns this half" per the engine's team-relative counter (kickoff events like Riot move it), not the literal turn number.

### Implementation sketches

**R1 — Stranded-safe-activations on turnover.** Hook the existing turnover event. Iterate own roster: `!activated && !stunned && no_forced_trait_roll(p)` (exclude Bone Head / Really Stupid / Animal Savagery / Unchannelled Fury / Blood Lust / Take Root — all in `skills.yaml`) and either `prone && MA>=3` (free stand-up, legal even while marked) or `standing && MA>=1 && tz_count(p)==0` (at least one roll-free square — entering a TZ is free, only leaving costs). All fields are existing engine state (activation flags, stance bits, TZ map). Penalize only on turnover so successful orderings — blitz-first lane openings, frenzy chains, desperation plays — are never constrained; this is the key trick that keeps the knob from hard-coding a strict activation order. Anti-tic option if evals show junk activations: count players who activated but moved zero squares as still stranded (costs you legitimate stand-still screens; only enable if needed).

**R2 — Stand-up discipline.** Same predicate as R1's prone clause, evaluated at **voluntary** turn end (all desired activations done / coach passes). R1 and R2 partition turn endings — turnover vs voluntary — so they can never double-fire on one turn. Exclude players stunned earlier this same turn (cannot activate). Zero-cost remedy, zero farming surface; the cheapest, safest knob in the set.

**R3 — Send-off penalty.** Fire on final dugout removal only — after Argue the Call and Bribe steps per `inducements.yaml` (a bribe save on 2+ fires nothing). Covers natural-double fouls, Biased Referee Close Scrutiny 5+, and secret-weapon bans if they share the event. Strictly one-sided: never reward the opponent's send-offs (Biased Referee + prone-bait makes a symmetric version farmable in self-play). This completes the fouling EV that is currently lopsided — the injury knob pays the foul's upside (+0.15 KO/CAS) while the downside (player gone for the whole game, worse than a KO) is invisible, which mildly subsidizes foul-spam today. With cost and benefit both priced, foul selection (assists, target AV, Sneaky Git, clock) is left to the policy. If foul rate collapses in eval, shrink toward −0.10 before removing.

**R4 — Final-turn conversion.** At end of the team's final turn of a half (team-relative counter): replay the turn's decision points; if at any point a standing un-stunned carrier had a BFS path of ≤ MA squares to the endzone with every step outside enemy TZs and no GFI/dodge/pickup/pass, and the half ended TD-less, charge −0.5. One predicate catches both failure modes: stalled one turn too long, and "took an optional risky action first that turned over before walking it in." Never fires mid-half — non-scoring while in range *is* correct stalling. Ship the zero-roll tier only; a GFI-only tier at −0.2 is a later option. Sized at −0.5 (not −1.0) so entering range and scoring (+1 net ≥ +0.5) strictly dominates avoidance (0), with room for the rare expert exception (fearing a one-turn-score reply).

**R5 — Reckless-block surcharge.** At `BlockDiceRolled`, compute p(attacker down) from the engine's own resolution tables — per-die red fraction 2/6 (1/6 with Block/Wrestle), attacker-choice p = f^n, defender-choice p = 1−(1−f)^n — so Brawler/Pro/Dauntless/Juggernaut/Wrestle come along for free (Wrestle counts as Block for turnover math, not knockdown value). If a team re-roll is legally usable and the actor lacks Loner, p_eff = p²; Loner X+: p_eff = p·(P(pass)·p + P(fail)). Evaluate ST/assists **at roll time**, not declaration (chain-pushes change the dice). Penalty −0.25·max(0, p_eff − 0.03) gives exactly 0 for doctrinally clean blocks (2d+Block, 1d+Block+RR) and ≈−0.13 for the worst red dice — this continuously prices the entire GRUMBBL execution ladder (3d+Block > … > red dice) with one formula and no discrete rules. Exemptions: team's final activation of the turn and last turn of a half (both engine-detectable; both blessed verbatim by bbtactics/NAF). Do **not** also ship the discrete uphill-block penalty — R5 is its strict generalization. Monitor block/blitz volume and re-roll spend rate as canaries.

**R6 — Carrier exposure.** At each settled own-turn end with an own standing carrier (reuse the possession-knob hook): BFS each standing opponent's legal movement (MA + blitz, TZ-exit squares labeled as dodge costs, GFIs allowed as non-dodge dice) for `min_dodges` to end adjacent to the carrier. Charge −0.05 if carrier is marked or `min_dodges == 0`; optionally −0.02 if exactly 1; **never** a positive for safe (subsidizes turtling — the draw −0.5 and TD terms must stay the only scoring pressure). Formation-agnostic by construction: tight cage, loose cage, screen wall, and sideline bunker all score by the same access-cost metric; never count diagonal cage-corners. Exempt when the carrier is within MA of the opposing endzone (scoring dashes are deliberately exposed). This is the single most valuable credit-assignment fix in the set: it charges the positional mistake on the turn it is made instead of waiting for the −0.5 sack a turn later.

**R6 refinement (Alex, 2026-06-15): graduated + adversarial exposure — two staged extensions to ship AFTER R6v1.**

The table-row spec above is R6v1: a near-binary floor (0-dodges-or-marked = −0.05, optionally 1-dodge = −0.02). Alex's framing — *"the first thing we should do is make it so no dodges can get you"* — is exactly R6v1's job, and it should ship first, unchanged, per §5's A/B-3. But two real gaps remain, staged as v1.5 and v2 so neither blocks v1:

- **R6v1.5 — graduated/probability-weighted exposure (same BFS, richer scoring function).** v1's tiers (0 / 1 / "safe") collapse a real continuum: 1-dodge access is *categorically* more dangerous than 3-dodges or a GFI-gated path, because the opponent's chance of actually converting the threat differs enormously (a single 3+/4+ dodge, possibly Dodge-skill-rerolled, vs. three independent dodges compounding failure — 0.83 vs ~0.58 for 5/6-each — vs. a path needing a GFI, which carries its OWN turnover risk for the *attacker*, making the opponent less willing to even try). The fix: instead of dodge-count tiers, price `−scale · P(best opponent reaches+dislodges the carrier this turn)`, computed the same way `k_kd`/`k_value`/`k_ball` already price block-EV — per-roll success probabilities (AG, Dodge/Tackle skill adjustments), compounded across the path's rolls, taking the MAX over candidate opponents. This unifies R6 with the existing exposure-pricing family (same "probability at declaration/settlement, dice-independent" philosophy as §"Dice outcomes carry no shaping" in the live header) and naturally reproduces Alex's ordering (0-dodge ≫ 1-dodge > 3-dodge > GFI-gated ≈ 0) without hand-picked tier constants. Same BFS as v1 — only the scoring function changes — so this is a low-risk in-place refinement once v1's canaries are read.

- **R6v2 — adversarial/displaceable-screener reachability (harder; separate iteration).** v1/v1.5's BFS treats squares occupied by *friendly* pieces as fixed obstacles. They aren't: a screening piece adjacent to an opponent can itself be **blocked and displaced**, and `BB_SK_GRAB` (verified in `proc_block.c:447` — Grab cancels the defender's Side Step, so the GRABBING attacker chooses the push square) lets the opponent *deliberately* push the screener out of the lane. Alex's example — a lone Dodge-skill Halfling "providing" a nominal 1-dodge buffer, standing next to a Block/Mighty-Blow/Grab Chaos Warrior about to throw 3 red dice — is not actually 1-dodge-exposure; the Warrior clears the Halfling first (high P(down), Grab picks the direction that opens the lane), and the *true* exposure is closer to R6's 0-dodge tier. Correct treatment: for any friendly piece whose removal/displacement would reduce another player's `min_dodges`, identify adjacent opponents who could block it, run the existing block-EV routine (`bb_blockev`) for "does this block's likely outcome open the lane," and fold `P(screener cleared) × P(then reach carrier via the newly-opened path)` into the v1.5 probability. This is a genuine 1-ply lookahead — bounded (only screeners whose removal changes `min_dodges`, only their adjacent opponents, reusing `bb_blockev` rather than a new EV engine) but a materially bigger build than v1/v1.5, and at SETTLED-TURN-END timing rather than `k_kd`'s block-declaration timing, so it can't just reuse that computation's call site. **Ship as its own iteration with its own canary**: does the v1/v1.5-trained policy learn to rely on fragile single-piece screens that a competent opponent would blow through (visible as e.g. high carrier-exposed-turn-rate-by-v1.5-accounting but high ACTUAL sack rate — a divergence between the *priced* exposure and the *true* one)? If v1.5 alone already drives sack rate down via OTHER signals (k_kd pricing the screener's own removal already discourages thin screens for unrelated reasons), v2's marginal value may be small — gate it on evidence, per §5's "remove or shrink, never add a counter-knob" rule applied in reverse (don't BUILD a counter-refinement without a trip-wire showing it's needed).

**R7 — Conceded-2D contact.** Same settled hook. For each own standing player adjacent to ≥1 standing opponent, run the engine's existing block-dice computation (ST + assists, current positions, no hypothetical movement) for the best block any single adjacent opponent gets; count ≥2-dice cases. The dice formulation — not raw adjacency — is load-bearing: deliberate marking (opposing carrier, receivers, LOS scrum, clock-burning mass-basing) is normally arranged as 1D contact and costs nothing. Carve-outs: player adjacent to the opposing ball carrier ("very rarely a reason not to mark the carrier"); last turn of half (opponent gets no turn).

**R8 — Surf-zone exposure.** Same settled hook; pure push-arc geometry the engine already computes for block resolution. Tier A: edge-column player where all legal push destinations from an adjacent (or no-dodge-blitz-reachable) opponent's block are off-pitch. Tier B: one square from edge vs Frenzy or chain-push-occupied inboard squares. Skill suppression for Side Step / Stand Firm is the doctrine's own stated exception ("unless they are protected") and preserves the expert "make the pitch wider" tech. The reach requirement (not mere edge proximity) is essential — an unengaged sideline player costs nothing, so wide-zone defense stays viable.

**R9 — No greed re-rolls.** Trigger on team-re-roll spend (not skill re-rolls) where the pre-re-roll block pool contained a selectable zero-turnover face: any Push, Defender Down/Stumbles (net of Dodge/Tackle), or Both Down with attacker Block/Wrestle. Engine sees the pre-re-roll pool, both skill sets, and the re-roll source — exact predicate. Exempt blocks/blitzes on the opposing ball carrier (re-rolling a push to fish for the sack is the canonical correct violation). Flat −0.05 keeps crowd-surf and last-turn-lane re-rolls affordable. Do not add any reward for holding *or* spending re-rolls — both directions are farmable, and unused re-rolls die at the half anyway (May-2025 FAQ).

**R10 — Carrier dice hygiene.** Trigger on dodge/rush roll resolution where the active player holds the ball; charge on attempt, outcome-independent (the −0.5 possession term already punishes the bad outcome; this fixes credit for the 5/6 of risky dodges that happen to succeed). Waive when the carrier began the activation marked (forced escape is taught doctrine). A 2+ rush into the endzone stays +0.97 net, so desperation scores are never suppressed. Watch one distortion: if handoff-then-dodge becomes a routing trick around the tax, that is the ball-juggling pathology — address by fixing the possession-gain trigger (see Interactions), not by adding a transfer tax.

**R11 — Clock-aware potential Φ.** The only positive-capable knob, safe because it is potential-based (Ng/Harada/Russell: policy-invariant at γ≈1 with Φ(terminal)=0). Feature table: score_diff clipped ±2 × own turns left (0–8) × opponent turns left (0–8) × half × settled possession ∈ {ours, theirs, loose} — ~10³ cells, fit by counts or logistic regression on the already-curated FUMBBL corpus. Emit ΔΦ on the same settled boundaries as the possession knob; enforce Φ_A = −Φ_B for zero-sum. This single term makes stalling, the fast equalizer when trailed, *and* the quick 2-3-turn score when leading 1-0 receiving in H2 all emerge from one signal — the doctrine is conditional and only a margin-and-clock-aware value captures all branches without hard-coding any. Hazards are integration-shaped, not farming-shaped: see Interactions §4. This is the heaviest item; ship in phase 3, not v1.

## 3. Rejected (rejection is the result)

- **Per-knockdown reward (+0.02).** Worst farming profile examined: symmetric in self-play, collapses training into LOS punch-fests that ignore the ball — the exact NAF-warned failure ("win on casualties, lose the game"). Removal value already paid stochastically by injury ±0.15.
- **Any positive per-foul / foul-frequency / target-threat term.** Foul frequency is the community's main *style* axis (roster-conditional), failing the universality bar; declarations are near-free so a positive is a 16-turn farm; double-pays the injury knob.
- **Ball-transfer tax (pass −0.02 / handoff −0.01).** Highest strategy-suppression risk surveyed — hard-codes bash style against legitimate agility relay/passing play and 1TTD setups; the doctrine's own framing ("emergency measure") means experts pass when needed. Failed transfers already feed −0.5. Revisit only if logging shows pathological ball-juggling, and first fix the possession trigger (§4.1).
- **Guarded-loose-ball credit (+0.03/episode).** Most exploitable positive in the raw set; episode-cycling is only net-negative by a tunable inequality that retuning could break. The possession terms plus the Secure-the-Ball action's mechanical 2+ already encode the value.
- **Kick placement / touchback-risk penalty.** Deep-vs-short is matchup-conditional (fails universality); the surviving touchback kernel is one decision per drive whose consequences Φ already credits. First cut by its own researcher.
- **Stall bonuses, TD-timing scalars, "held ball while leading" rent.** Doctrine is conditional; unconditional versions are farmable by refusing correct early scores. Entirely subsumed by R11's potential form.
- **Cage shape / column spacing / screen templates / one-turn-score-denial setup.** Rewarding formations, not fundamentals — direct violation of constraint (c); guides themselves generalize away from the shapes. R6–R8 reward the underlying invariants.
- **Raw players-in-opposing-TZ penalty.** Mis-penalizes correct marking constantly; teaches pathological disengagement. R7's conceded-dice form is the fix.
- **Re-roll budgeting beyond R9** ("save one for the scoring turn"). "Trivial failure" and "threatened ball" are future-trajectory value judgments — precisely what the value network should learn; any concrete proxy encodes strategy.
- **Block target selection** (hit the killer / the Skeleton not the Zombie). Threat-contextual value judgment; sources conflict on the predicate; leave to injury/possession/win signals.
- **Spread-vs-cluster shaping.** Genuinely contested by roster archetype (agility scatters, bash clusters); universal kernel already in R7.
- **Kick-vs-receive coin toss, bribe/Argue micro-decisions, fine-grained block-order enforcement, sweeper/safety, "don't follow up", skill-acquisition meta.** Either one decision per match, contested between top sources, ill-defined counterfactuals, or positional strategy — all left to the policy.

## 4. Interactions with existing knobs

1. **Possession (+0.1/−0.5) — pre-flight check, do this first.** Verify +0.1 fires only on *team-level* settled possession transitions, never on within-team handoffs/passes; if a handoff re-triggers it, ball-cycling is a farm today, independent of anything in this memo. R6 is the deliberate leading indicator of −0.5 (same hook, fires the turn *before* the sack) — keep exposure-penalty + sack-loss well under one TD per incident (−0.05 + −0.5 is fine).
2. **Injury (±0.15).** R5 and R7 suppress block volume and therefore +0.15 income — injury-inflicted rate is the designated passivity canary for the whole positional/risk set. R3 deliberately mirrors injury-taken (send-off ≈ CAS without permanence). R8 is the positional precursor of surf-induced injury-taken, same pattern as R7 vs blocks. Never mirror R3 or knockdowns as positives (self-play farming).
3. **TD (±1) and draw (−0.5).** R4 sized so converting strictly dominates avoiding range; R6's endzone-sprint exemption and R10's tiny size keep scoring dashes untaxed; draw −0.5 is the systemic anti-turtle bound for all penalty-only positional knobs.
4. **Win (±3) and Φ.** Φ(terminal)=0 keeps win ±3 the only terminal signal; Φ must be anti-symmetric between agents. **Either exclude the possession feature from Φ or retire the possession knob when Φ ships** — running both double-counts every possession swing. If Φ ships, do not additionally scale TD by turn (subsumed). Refresh P̂ from self-play checkpoints later so the human prior doesn't become a permanent drag.
5. **Intra-set partitions.** R1 (turnover ends) and R2 (voluntary ends) never co-fire. R5 replaces, never stacks with, a discrete uphill-block rule. Combined settled-state positional charge (R6+R7+R8) capped ≈ −0.3/turn. R3 + second-wave foul knobs stack against a thin upside (58% of foul outcomes are stuns earning 0) — if eval foul rate → ~0: drop fouler-expendability first, then shrink R3 to −0.10.
6. **Setup (±0.25).** No interactions identified.

## 5. Experiment order

**Eval harness first:** per-checkpoint dashboards for block/blitz volume, dodge & GFI rates **per roster archetype** (agility vs bash — the elf-suppression canary), foul rate, send-off rate, re-roll spend, stranded-activation count, carrier-exposed-turn rate, sack rate, stall length, and scoreline distribution vs the NAF 259k-game anchor (2-1/2-0/1-0/1-1 = 74% of results) plus FUMBBL replay action-distribution baselines.

1. **A/B-1 (accounting & ordering, near-zero hazard):** baseline vs baseline + {R1, R2, R3, R4}. Pure penalty-on-blunder knobs with zero-cost remedies; no style risk. Expect: fewer stranded activations, foul EV sane (rate neither spiking nor collapsing), no untaken walk-ins. Cheapest implementation (existing event hooks + one BFS for R4).
2. **A/B-2 (dice discipline):** winner + {R5, R9, R10}. Canaries: block volume, dodge rate on agility rosters, re-roll spend rate (hoarding check), injury-inflicted rate. If agility play depresses, halve k on R5 before touching exemptions.
3. **A/B-3 (positional trio, shared settled hook + one TZ-BFS routine):** winner + {R6, R7, R8} under the combined −0.3 cap. Canaries: contact rate, sack rate against (should drop), stall length, surf losses. R6 is the priority of the three.
4. **Phase 3:** Φ (R11), retiring or feature-excluding the possession knob; calibrate against the NAF scoreline table. Then second-wave items only on the evidence conditions in §2, each as its own ablation.

Rule for all phases: when a canary trips, **remove or shrink the offending knob — never add a counter-knob.**
