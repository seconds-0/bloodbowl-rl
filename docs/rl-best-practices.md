## 1. EXECUTIVE: the 5 highest-value holes, ranked by (impact on escaping the avoidance equilibrium × implementation cost)

### 1. PufferLib hard-clamps rewards to [-1, 1] — our reward hierarchy never existed
**Finding:** Both trainers clamp rewards *before* advantage computation: `vendor/PufferLib/pufferlib/torch_pufferl.py:411` (`rew = self.rewards.T.contiguous().clamp(-1, 1)`) and `vendor/PufferLib/src/pufferlib.cu:1509-1510` (`clamp_precision_kernel`). Our `reward_win = 3.0` in `puffer/config/bloodbowl.ini` is clamped to **+1.0**, so the agent has trained under Win(±1) = TD(±1), not the documented Win(±3) ≫ TD(±1). A game-winning TD (+1 TD, +3 win → +4) clamps to +1, making the win bonus invisible exactly when it matters. The effective win-vs-draw gap was 1.5, not 3.5 — directly favoring the draw equilibrium. The clamp also breaks the [Ng et al. 1999] PBRS telescoping invariance on any step where shaping + event rewards saturate.
**Action:** Rescale the reward design into [-1,1] (win=1.0, td=0.3, draw=-0.15, micro-rewards ≤0.05) or add a one-line clamp-removal patch alongside `training/torch_pufferl_bcreg.patch`. Then **re-baseline**: the "pure outcome rewards converge to 0-0" conclusion was measured under a different reward function than documented. **Effort: hours. Do this first.**

### 2. Demo-state reset curriculum from FUMBBL replays (the chess "FEN curriculum" pattern)
**Literature:** PufferLib's own flagship 2-player selfplay env (chess) escapes sparse-reward basins not with shaping but by starting **90% of episodes from non-initial positions** (`vendor/PufferLib/config/chess.ini` `fen_curric_pct = 0.9`; `ocean/chess/chess.h:2318-2333`, `binding.c:106-133`). Same mechanism solved Montezuma with plain PPO ([Salimans & Chen 2018](https://arxiv.org/abs/1812.03381)), and **beat both reward shaping and BC head-to-head in a stochastic zero-sum multiplayer game** ([Backplay, Resnick et al. 2018](https://arxiv.org/abs/1807.06919), Pommerman). Success-band gating per [Florensa et al. 2017](https://arxiv.org/abs/1707.05300).
**Why it fits us:** Potential-free — nothing to anneal, nothing for PPO to erode. Our Layer-7 FUMBBL/FFB replay differential harness already reconstructs engine states from human games, and memcpy reset makes restoration O(1). Episodes start *inside* scoring situations (carrier 3 squares out, drive in progress), so "convert this position to a TD against a live league opponent" is trained on true reward only. Uses demo **states, not actions** — sidesteps the BC erosion problem entirely.
**Action:** Dump mid-game states from human replays into a state file; add a `state_curric_pct` reset path to the BB env mirroring chess's `SHARED_FEN_CURRICULUM` pattern in `binding.c` (single shared load in `my_vec_init`). Anneal start points backward from near-TD states (Salimans-Chen). Keep a held-out from-kickoff eval to detect critic distribution skew. **Effort: ~2-4 days.**

### 3. The frozen bank is homogeneous — seed it with scorers and add an exploiter slot
**Literature:** League methods work because the pool is heterogeneous **in objective**, not in checkpoint age. AlphaStar's exploiters (reset to supervised params, trained vs the current main agent) measurably reduced exploitability ([Vinyals et al. 2019, Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-019-1724-z)); a curriculum-then-league pipeline was required for full-game football ([TiZero, AAMAS 2023](https://arxiv.org/abs/2302.07515)); cheap exploiters bootstrapped from the victim's value head exist ([Minimax Exploiter, Ubisoft 2023](https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.17190)). Chess in PufferLib anchors selfplay to a fixed human-like external opponent (`CHESS_MODE_MAIA`, `chess.h:360-364`).
**Our gap:** Every member of our 200-entry pool is a passive clone from one lineage in one basin. No reweighting of a homogeneous pool produces anti-passive gradient — this is exactly why Elo-gated swaps didn't prevent collapse. Worse, in the all-draw regime `hist` winrate sits at 0.5 < `swap_winrate` 0.55, so **all our swaps are timeout swaps and the pool curriculum is inert**.
**Action:** (a) Pin BC-init, BC-final, and the curriculum-stage-1 checkpoint (tds 0.185 — currently the only entity that scores) as **permanent bank members** exempt from swap/eviction — `num_frozen_banks` supports up to 8 banks (`selfplay.py:145-149`); a small patch skips swap logic for designated banks. Once 0-0 reliably loses to a bank member that picks up the ball, passivity stops being locally optimal. (b) Add one exploiter slot: periodically fork a policy initialized from the curriculum/BC checkpoint, train it only vs the frozen current main (shaping at full strength — exploiters are disposable), gate into the bank at win/TD thresholds. (c) Log `pool/winrate_at_swap_bank_*` to confirm the inert-pool diagnosis. **Effort: (a)+(c) ~1 day; (b) ~1 week.**

### 4. The BC-regularized arm anchors on the WRONG states
**Literature:** Every successful anchor system computes the imitation loss on **the student's own on-policy states** against a **frozen SL policy network**, not on the raw dataset: [Kickstarting, Schmitt et al. 2018](https://arxiv.org/abs/1803.03835) (teacher queried on student trajectories; constant λ plateaus, PBT-adapted λ best), [QDagger, Agarwal et al. NeurIPS 2022](https://arxiv.org/abs/2206.01626) (wean λ as a function of student/teacher performance ratio, works with suboptimal teachers), [piKL/DiL-piKL, Jacob et al. ICML 2022](https://arxiv.org/abs/2112.07544) / [Bakhtin et al. ICLR 2023](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.05492) (λ ∈ {1e-4…1e-1}; distributional λ beats one annealed scalar), AlphaStar (continual KL to the supervised policy, never annealed for main agents).
**Our gap:** Aux masked-CE on the fixed 24K human pairs constrains the policy only at 24K dataset states — it cannot apply pressure in the ball-avoidance states the rollouts actually visit, and at high weight it memorizes 24K decisions rather than regularizing toward human BB strategy.
**Action:** Freeze the BC policy as a teacher network; replace the dataset-CE term with per-head, mask-aware `KL(π_BC(·|s_t) ‖ π_θ(·|s_t))` on PPO rollout states. Wean adaptively on eval-Elo(student)/eval-Elo(BC) (QDagger), or sample λ per-episode from {0, low, high} (DiL-piKL). Secondary: AlphaStar's biggest ablation win was **not** the KL but z-statistic conditioning + pseudo-rewards for matching human strategy statistics — extract per-game stats (pickups attempted, blocks thrown, drive tempo) from FUMBBL replays as a follow-up arm. **Effort: ~2-3 days (the arm is live now — fix before burning 10B steps).**

### 5. Credit cannot reach the TD: GAE λ=0.85 + collapsed critic
**Literature:** λ<1 introduces bias *only where the value function is inaccurate* ([Schulman et al. 2016, GAE](https://arxiv.org/abs/1506.02438)); γ is the single most important hyperparameter and λ≈0.9 is the robust default ([Andrychowicz et al. 2020](https://arxiv.org/abs/2006.05990)); dense auxiliary value targets were KataGo's single biggest selfplay efficiency win, ~50x over Leela Zero ([Wu 2019](https://arxiv.org/abs/1902.10565)).
**Our gap (mechanistic account of scoring being pruned):** at γ=0.995, λ=0.85, the direct GAE credit window is 1/(1−γλ) ≈ **6.5 decisions**; beyond that all credit flows through the value function — which in the 0-0 basin is flat (V≈0 everywhere). Rare TD events propagate ~6 decisions and vanish into a collapsed critic. Our critic is maximally inaccurate exactly around scoring sequences, i.e., the regime where λ=0.85 bias is worst.
**Action:** Raise λ to 0.95 for basin-escape runs (direct window ≈ 38 decisions). Add KataGo-style auxiliary value heads — final TD differential, ball-possessing team k turns ahead, end-of-drive square ownership — all computable from existing rollouts; a critic forced to predict possession cannot collapse to constant zero. Anneal γ **upward mid-run** (0.995 → 0.999 → 0.9997, OpenAI Five precedent, [Berner et al. 2019](https://arxiv.org/abs/1912.06680)) gated on value explained-variance; raise BPTT `horizon` alongside so the recurrent core can condition at the horizon the discount values. **Effort: λ is a config change; aux heads ~2-3 days.**

Near-misses that just missed the top 5: a Protein hyperparameter sweep (we have never swept; chess's swept ent_coef is 5x ours — §7) and the standardized eval suite (§9, which would have exposed 0-0 as fragile long before the cross-population tournament).

## 2. Selfplay leagues & population dynamics

**What the literature says.** Naive independent selfplay converges to jointly-correlated local equilibria, not robust strategies ([Lanctot et al., NeurIPS 2017](https://mlanctot.info/files/papers/nips17-psro.pdf) — Joint Policy Correlation). When the objective-pursuing action sequence has negative EV at low competence, "never do it" is a stable attractor — Pommerman agents learned to never place bombs ([Skynet, Gao et al. 2019](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1905.01360)); sparse-reward selfplay soccer fails without dense shaping/population methods ([Liu et al., ICLR 2019](https://arxiv.org/abs/1902.07151)); GRF needed curriculum-then-league ([TiZero](https://arxiv.org/abs/2302.07515)) or demonstrations ([TiKick](https://arxiv.org/abs/2110.04507)). Populations must span the non-transitive disc to climb the transitive axis ([Czarnecki et al., NeurIPS 2020, "Spinning Tops"](https://proceedings.neurips.cc/paper_files/paper/2020/file/ca172e964907a97d5ebd876bfdd4adbd-Paper.pdf)). The cures, in AlphaStar's ablation-validated order: PFSP > FSP > naive SP; exploiters further reduce exploitability ([Vinyals et al. 2019](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-019-1724-z): main agents 35% SP / 50% PFSP / 15% forgotten+exploiters; f_hard(x)=(1−x)^p weighting; main exploiters reset to supervised params). OpenAI Five used 80% current-self / 20% past checkpoints with dynamically decayed opponent quality scores ([Berner et al. 2019](https://arxiv.org/abs/1912.06680), Appendix N). DeepNash's R-NaD ([Perolat et al., Science 2022](https://arxiv.org/abs/2206.15378)) cures *cycling*, not *passivity* — wrong tool for our failure mode.

**What we do.** Frozen-bank pool (`max_size=200`, `swap_winrate=0.55`, Elo-gated swaps, `frozen_bank_pct=0.1`), one lineage, opponents sampled with mild staleness weighting (`selfplay.py:27-32` excludes the 5 newest, weights (i+1)^0.5).

**The gap.** (a) Homogeneous pool — diversity of checkpoint timestamp, not of training objective. (b) In a ~100%-draw regime, winrate pins at 0.5 → all swaps are timeouts → pool growth is cadence-only and the curriculum is dead. (c) No exploiters, so nothing ever punishes ball-avoidance. (d) Sampling is uniform-ish, not PFSP. Note chess's Protein-swept pool values for comparison: `max_size=500`, `swap_winrate=0.6`, `snapshot_interval=1B`, `frozen_bank_pct` swept up to 0.5.

**Recommended changes.**
1. Permanent heterogeneous bank seeds (BC-init, BC-final, curriculum-stage-1) — see Executive §3. *~1 day.*
2. PFSP sampling over the bank: p(j) ∝ (1−winrate vs j)² with a floor probability; add a TD-conceded term so "draws 100% but never threatened" doesn't count as mastered. *~1 day.*
3. Single-GPU exploiter phases: alternate main-agent training with forked exploiter runs (init from curriculum/BC checkpoint, trained only vs frozen main, shaping on, gated into bank at ≥70% win or TD threshold). Optionally bootstrap the exploiter's critic from the frozen main's value head ([Minimax Exploiter](https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.17190)). Run exploiter phases at γ ≥ 0.9995 so TD credit fits the horizon. *~1 week.*
4. Bank admission rule: candidates must demonstrate nonzero TD rate vs the bank, not just Elo gain — prevents refilling with passive clones. *Hours.*
5. Standing exploitability probe (eval, not trainer): every K steps train a short-budget best response vs the frozen main; report its win/TD rate. This distinguishes "robust 0-0" from "degenerate 0-0" ([Timbers et al., IJCAI 2022](https://arxiv.org/abs/2004.09677); [Gleave et al., ICLR 2020](https://arxiv.org/abs/1905.10615); [Wang et al., ICML 2023](https://arxiv.org/abs/2211.00241) — 97% win vs superhuman KataGo). Success for the whole program = exploitability falling, not Elo rising.
6. A 1-2-ply afterstate/expectimax searcher over the trained value net (TD-Gammon 2.x pattern; [Lanctot et al. 2013, MC \*-minimax](https://arxiv.org/abs/1304.6057)) is a cheap, much-stronger league opponent and eval gate — train/test compute are exchangeable ([Jones 2021](https://arxiv.org/abs/2104.03113)). *~3-5 days; engine is deterministic and fast, memcpy scratch states make this easy.*

Defer indefinitely: full PSRO as the trainer (each iteration = a full RL run + O(n²) payoff matrix), R-NaD (replaces the PPO loss; cures cycling we don't have). Use PSRO's *evaluation* machinery (empirical win matrix → restricted Nash over the bank) cheaply instead.

## 3. Imitation anchoring (BC + RL hybrids)

**What the literature says.** Initialization-only BC erodes under RL — Kickstarting exists precisely to avoid it; distill from a teacher on student trajectories instead ([Schmitt et al. 2018](https://arxiv.org/abs/1803.03835)). Adaptive weaning on student/teacher performance ratio beats fixed schedules ([QDagger](https://arxiv.org/abs/2206.01626)). Small/imperfect anchors regularize rather than overfit iff λ is small, the anchor is a generalizing network queried on on-policy states, and λ is weaned or mixed ([piKL](https://arxiv.org/abs/2112.07544); [DiL-piKL](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.05492) — agent plays λ=1e-4 but λ=1e-2 on the opening turn where the anchor is most trustworthy). AlphaStar: continual supervised-KL for main agents (never annealed; league resets supply pressure), hard anchor on the opening (~first 4 min action-type KL), and z-statistic pseudo-rewards — the biggest single ablation gain. Winners-only demo filtering matters: AlphaStar's fine-tune on winning MMR>6200 replays lifted Elite-bot winrate 87%→96%; MPPO breaks if negative-return demos are included ([arXiv 2506.16995](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.16995)). DQfD worked from 5.5K-30K transitions but with margin loss + permanently retained demos ([Hester et al. 2017](https://arxiv.org/abs/1704.03732)). MimicBot (Bot Bowl III winner — our game) used 400K scripted pairs, interleaved BC/A2C updates, then cold cutoff at only 12M steps ([Pezzotti 2021](https://arxiv.org/abs/2108.09478)). Zero-start-state iid training of a recurrent policy causes severe hidden-state staleness; fix with stored-state/burn-in or full-sequence training ([R2D2, Kapturowski et al., ICLR 2019](https://openreview.net/pdf?id=r1lyTjAqYX)).

**What we do.** 24K human decision pairs; BC warm-start (eroded); BC-regularized PPO with aux masked-CE on the fixed pairs, cosine-annealed coefficient (just launched); iid zero-state BC on a recurrent MinGRU policy.

**The gap.** (a) Anchor applied at dataset states, not on-policy states (Executive §4). (b) Wall-clock cosine anneal instead of performance-adaptive weaning. (c) iid zero-state BC trains a policy that only knows how to act with empty memory — PPO then operates with accumulated hidden state, so the BC prior partially lives off the manifold; plausible contributor to erosion. (d) Corpus is 1-2 orders of magnitude below BC-as-strong-policy range and unfiltered by outcome.

**Recommended changes.**
1. On-policy KL vs frozen BC teacher, per-head and mask-aware (Executive §4). *~2-3 days.*
2. Sequence-BC: reconstruct contiguous decision sequences per replay; train with full-episode unrolls (MinGRU's parallel scan makes this cheap) or windows with ≥40-decision burn-in; zero state only at true episode starts. Apply the same to the anchor loss if any dataset-state CE is kept. *~2 days.*
3. Scale the corpus: harvest more FUMBBL replays (a few thousand replays ≈ ~1M pairs), filter to high-rated players, fine-tune the anchor on the **winning team's decisions only**. *~3-5 days, mostly harvest throttling.*
4. Phase-dependent λ: anchor hard where human data is dense and trustworthy (setup, kickoff formations, early-drive ball retrieval), light elsewhere — the BB analog of AlphaStar's opening-only KL and DiL-piKL's first-turn λ. *~1 day on top of #1.*
5. Follow-up arm: z-statistic conditioning + pseudo-rewards (Hamming/edit distance to human per-game strategy stats, each active with prob 25%, separate value heads) — a principled non-decaying version of our potential curriculum. *~1 week.*
6. Go-Explore as a demo factory: memcpy-reset + cell archive (ball-x bucket × carrier × score diff × half × turn) against top-K bank members; extract TD trajectories that score against ≥2 bank members; feed them to the anchor loss as in-distribution, league-current demos that regenerate as the bank evolves ([Ecoffet et al., Nature 2021](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-020-03157-3)). ~1B exploration steps ≈ minutes at our throughput. No published Go-Explore-in-selfplay precedent — value is entirely conditional on the consumption mechanism (BC-reg/SIL/resets). *~2-4 days.*

## 4. Action factorization (3 masked heads {30, 33, 391})

**What the literature says.** Hard masking is sound and load-bearing — a state-dependent differentiable transform with valid policy gradients; penalties don't scale ([Huang & Ontañón 2020/2022](https://arxiv.org/abs/2006.14171)). Independent heads over a shared trunk are proven at scale: OpenAI Five, [Gym-µRTS](https://arxiv.org/abs/2105.13807), and Tencent's MOBA agent which *deliberately decoupled* heads and beat pros ([Ye et al., AAAI 2020](https://arxiv.org/abs/1912.09729)). Known failure modes: (1) marginal recombination — a product of marginals can't represent correlated joint optima ([Tavakoli et al. 2018, BDQ](https://arxiv.org/abs/1711.08946); [Tang et al., NeurIPS 2022](https://openreview.net/forum?id=Jd70afzIvJ4) for bias-free conditions); (2) loss pollution from argument heads the chosen action type ignores — SC2LE masks unused argument heads out of the loss ([Vinyals et al. 2017](https://arxiv.org/abs/1708.04782)); AlphaStar went autoregressive to condition each argument on the previous ones.

**What we do.** Three independent heads with per-head legality masks over a shared MinGRU trunk (the published mitigation for recombination is already in place).

**The gap.** Unknown joint-invalidity rate; unused heads likely contribute noise to the PPO ratio and entropy.

**Recommended changes (diagnostic before surgery).**
1. Log the fraction of sampled joint actions that are jointly-invalid-but-marginally-valid, segmented by state type (handoffs, blitzes, scoring range). If <1-2%, recombination is a non-issue. *Hours.*
2. Mask unused argument heads out of log-prob and entropy in `sample_logits` (SC2LE practice). *Hours.*
3. If (1) is material: sequential conditional masking — sample head 1, recompute head-2 mask conditioned on the type, sample, recompute head-3 mask; the C engine can emit per-(type)/per-(type,player) masks as cheaply as the joint mask. Joint support becomes exactly the engine-legal set, no new parameters. *~2-3 days.*
4. Only on further evidence: lightweight autoregressive conditioning (embed sampled head-1 action into head-2/3 inputs); fallback exotic option is DouZero-style (state, action)-pair scoring over the legal list ([Zha et al., ICML 2021](https://arxiv.org/abs/2106.06135)). *Weeks — needs proof first.*

Never evaluate unmasked — behavior degrades when the mask is removed post-training (Huang & Ontañón).

## 5. Credit assignment, gamma, value learning

**What the literature says.** See Executive §5 for the core λ/γ/aux-target argument ([GAE](https://arxiv.org/abs/1506.02438); [Andrychowicz et al. 2020](https://arxiv.org/abs/2006.05990); [KataGo](https://arxiv.org/abs/1902.10565)). V-trace: ρ̄ = c̄ = 1.0 were best across IMPALA's sweeps; ρ̄ sets the value fixed point, c̄ only convergence speed — don't lower c̄ to "shorten" credit, λ does that with better-understood bias ([Espeholt et al. 2018](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1802.01561)). With imperfect value estimates, smaller training-γ acts as a regularizer (Amit et al., ICML 2020; Jiang et al., AAMAS 2015) — so anneal γ upward as the critic matures (OpenAI Five: 0.998→0.9997 mid-run) rather than starting high. Afterstate value factorization integrates dice noise out of the value target — the reason TD-Gammon worked ([Tesauro 1995](https://bkgm.com/articles/tesauro/tdl.html)) and the core of [Stochastic MuZero](https://openreview.net/forum?id=X6D9bAHhBQ1).

**What we do.** γ=0.995, λ=0.85, fused VTrace⊕GAE kernel (`pufferlib.cu:1240-1258`; with `replay_ratio=0.25, update_epochs=1` the ratios stay ≈1 and V-trace is nearly inert), `horizon=64` BPTT segments, single scalar value head.

**The gap.** Direct credit window ≈ 6.5 decisions against 300-900-decision episodes and multi-turn drives; critic collapsed to V≈0 in the draw regime, severing the only long-range credit conduit; vtrace clips were on the sweep list (wasted budget); afterstates unexploited despite the engine exposing decision → dice → decision natively.

**Recommended changes.**
1. λ 0.85 → 0.95 for basin-escape runs. *Config change.*
2. Aux value heads (TD diff, possession k-ahead, end-of-drive ownership, cas/KO counts). *~2-3 days.*
3. γ ladder annealed upward mid-run, gated on value explained-variance; raise `horizon` alongside (the MinGRU's carried state is the only credit channel past the BPTT truncation). *Config + monitoring.*
4. Keep vtrace_rho_clip = vtrace_c_clip = 1.0; remove from sweep space. *Config change.*
5. Aux afterstate value head: env emits a post-decision/pre-dice observation; train V(afterstate) alongside V(s) — lower-variance targets, GAE pipeline untouched. Also the substrate for the 1-2-ply eval searcher (§2.6). *~3-4 days.*

## 6. Shaping, curriculum, exploration

**What the literature says.** Time-varying potentials preserve policy invariance — our annealed coefficient is a dynamic PBRS *if* implemented inside the difference: F_t = γ·c_{t'}·Φ(s') − c_t·Φ(s) ([Devlin & Kudenko, AAMAS 2012](http://www.ifaamas.org/Proceedings/aamas2012/papers/2C_3.pdf)). PIES decays a shaping-influence coefficient to exactly zero before end of training and guarantees convergence to the env-optimal policy even from adversarial advice ([Behboudian et al. 2020](https://arxiv.org/abs/2011.01297)). PBRS ≡ Q-initialization ([Wiewiora 2003](https://arxiv.org/abs/1106.5267)) — cold removal is a critic shock, explaining our decay-to-zero. Φ(terminal) must be 0 in episodic settings ([Grześ, AAMAS 2017](https://www.ifaamas.org/Proceedings/aamas2017/pdfs/p565.pdf)). At λ<1, GAE is only unbiased if Φ = V*, so a shaping residue leaks non-invariantly into advantages — the potentials are slightly hackable until the anneal completes ([Schulman et al. 2016, §reward-shaping interpretation](https://arxiv.org/abs/1506.02438)). PBRS preserves the Nash set in stochastic games ([Devlin & Kudenko, AAMAS 2011](https://eprints.whiterose.ac.uk/id/eprint/75111/1/aamas11.pdf)) — shaping changes which equilibrium the dynamics find, which is exactly the right tool, because 0-0 is a *learning-dynamics attractor*, not a Nash equilibrium (unilateral scoring wins). Backgammon selfplay "just worked" because the rules force progress — stalling is illegal ([Pollack & Blair, NeurIPS 1996](http://papers.neurips.cc/paper/1292-why-did-td-gammon-work.pdf)); our carry potential is a synthetic forced-progress term restoring a structural property BB lacks. Bonus-based exploration (RND/ICM/pseudo-counts) gives no meaningful gain outside Montezuma-likes ([Taïga et al., ICLR 2020](https://arxiv.org/abs/2109.11052)) and attacks discovery, not retention — our policy *knows* the scoring states and unlearns the behavior. Prioritized Level Replay scores levels by positive value loss and replays informative ones ([Jiang et al., ICML 2021](https://arxiv.org/abs/2010.03934); [Robust PLR](https://arxiv.org/abs/2110.02439)); ACCEL mutates high-regret levels ([Parker-Holder et al., ICML 2022](https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.01302)).

**What we do.** Fetch-ball + carry-toward-endzone potentials; gradual anneal halving per 10B-step stage (stage 1 ended at tds 0.185, still climbing); event rewards (surf/casualty knobs) outside the PBRS class; procgen rosters sampled uniformly.

**The gap.** (a) Stage gates are wall-clock, not performance-gated — stage 1 was cut while still climbing. (b) Anneal implementation may scale the computed difference rather than the potential (invariance leak). (c) Φ(terminal) at drive end unaudited — the carry potential peaks exactly where the TD terminates the drive. (d) Event rewards are the unprotected hackable surface (casualty-farming punch-fest is a second mutual non-scoring equilibrium). (e) No prioritized sampling over rosters or reset states.

**Recommended changes.**
1. Gate anneal stages on TD-rate success bands (Florensa-style: anneal while in band, pause below), not step counts. Theory predicts stages can be *fast* once the policy reliably scores, because true reward takes over Φ's role. *Hours.*
2. Audit: Φ(terminal)=0 at drive/game end; anneal coefficient applied inside the PBRS difference; PBRS recomputed with the *training* γ whenever the γ ladder moves. *Hours.*
3. Finish the anneal at exactly 0, replacing the safety function of a permanent floor with structural mechanisms (reset curriculum §1.2, bank scorers/exploiters §1.3, BC anchor §1.4). Two reports disagree here — Pollack & Blair's forced-progress argument plus our cold-removal regression argue for a 10-20% permanent floor; [Skalse et al., NeurIPS 2022](https://arxiv.org/abs/2209.13085) (any non-constant proxy is hackable) plus the GAE-λ leak argue for zero. **Resolution: keep a floor only until at least one structural anti-passivity mechanism is live, then anneal to 0 with metric gates.**
4. Monitor proxy-vs-true divergence (shaped return vs TD/win) continuously — hacking phase-transitions with capability ([Pan et al., ICLR 2022](https://arxiv.org/abs/2201.03544)); scan [Krakovna's specification-gaming list](https://deepmind.google/blog/specification-gaming-the-flip-side-of-ai-ingenuity/) before adding any event knob. *Hours.*
5. One prioritized-reset sampler (PLR positive-value-loss scoring — we already compute GAE) serving both procgen roster seeds and the reset-state corpus (FUMBBL states + Go-Explore archive). ## 9. Do NOT do

1. **Do not evaluate or deploy unmasked.** Mask removal post-training degrades behavior ([Huang & Ontañón](https://arxiv.org/abs/2006.14171)).
2. **Do not reach for RND/ICM/NovelD to escape the basin.** No-gain outside Montezuma-likes ([Taïga et al. 2020](https://arxiv.org/abs/2109.11052)); our failure is retention, not discovery; novelty bonuses are spent on ball states early, then erosion proceeds identically; symmetric bonuses make the training game general-sum.
3. **Do not cold-remove shaping or any anchor.** PBRS ≡ Q-init; removal is a critic shock ([Wiewiora 2003](https://arxiv.org/abs/1106.5267)) — confirmed by our own regression. If the BC-anchor coefficient nears zero and erosion reappears, refresh the reference policy (R-NaD pattern, [Perolat et al. 2022](https://arxiv.org/abs/2206.15378)) rather than letting the anchor vanish.
4. **Do not park the shaping anneal at a permanent ε floor without a structural backstop.** Any non-constant proxy is hackable ([Skalse et al. 2022](https://arxiv.org/abs/2209.13085)); the GAE-λ leak makes carry-potential micro-farming weakly profitable until the anneal completes.
5. **Do not hard-CE the same 24K dataset states at high weight forever.** That memorizes 24K decisions, not human BB strategy; anchor on on-policy states with small/mixed λ (piKL/Kickstarting/QDagger).
6. **Do not punish stalling.** Waiting until turn 8 to score is *correct* Blood Bowl play; a hack-detector keyed on delay-to-score punishes optimal strategy.
7. **Do not gate curriculum stages on wall-clock steps.** Stage 1 was cut while tds was still climbing; gate on success-band metrics ([Florensa et al. 2017](https://arxiv.org/abs/1707.05300)).
8. **Do not start runs cold at γ=0.99975.** Smaller training-γ regularizes an immature critic (Amit et al., ICML 2020); anneal upward mid-run (OpenAI Five).
9. **Do not sweep or lower V-trace clips.** ρ̄ = c̄ = 1.0 ([IMPALA](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1802.01561)); λ is the principled credit-shortening knob.
10. **Do not use scalar Elo (or pool-internal winrate) as a primary metric.** Degenerate in draw-saturated regimes; unreliable in non-transitive regions ([Bertrand et al. 2023](https://arxiv.org/abs/2206.12301); [Czarnecki et al. 2020](https://arxiv.org/abs/2004.09468)). Keep the full cross-table as the primary artifact.
11. **Do not trust selfplay-internal dashboards as progress.** "Converged, 100% draws" was the dashboard's success state while the policy was degenerate. GRF, OpenAI Five, AlphaStar all report against fixed external references.
12. **Do not run the torch backend in bf16** (unstable per docs); do not trust cross-backend checkpoint evals until the issue-#579 hidden-state fix is applied locally.
13. **Do not build full PSRO/R-NaD as the trainer or PAIRED as the curriculum.** PSRO iterations cost a full RL run each; R-NaD cures cycling, not passivity, and replaces the PPO loss; PAIRED is unstable and dominated by Robust PLR ([Parker-Holder et al. 2022](https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.01302); [Dennis et al. 2020](https://arxiv.org/abs/2012.02096)).
14. **Do not let new reward components sum past the [-1,1] clamp** (until/unless the clamp is patched) — stacked event rewards silently truncate, breaking both the hierarchy and PBRS invariance.
15. **Do not seed-fix Go-Explore trajectories.** memcpy restore already gives exact return; seed-fixed dice trajectories are lucky-dice artifacts needing heavy robustification.

## 10. Standardized eval suite (run after EVERY experiment arm)

Single entry point (`tools/eval_suite.py`) → `eval_report.json` + cross-table heatmap. Engine speed makes everything cheap except C. Current `Log` struct (`puffer/bloodbowl/bloodbowl.h:106-127`) is selfplay-internal only — that is why 0-0 looked like convergence.

**A. Frozen reference gauntlet** (the comparability backbone — AlphaStar's fixed ablation-agent pattern, [Vinyals et al. 2019](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-019-1724-z)). Versioned immutable `eval_bank_v1`: random-legal; scripted heuristic (pickup→run greedy + mark-the-carrier, GrodBot-style — comparability with the [botbowl lineage](https://njustesen.github.io/botbowl/bot-bowl-iii.html)); BC-init; BC-final; curriculum-stage-1 best; best checkpoint per completed arm. Members are immutable; new strong checkpoints extend `eval_bank_v2`. **1000 games per opponent, paired**: same procgen teams + mirrored kick/receive (+ optionally mirrored seeds — fishtest pentanomial practice, [Fishtest mathematics](https://official-stockfish.github.io/docs/fishtest-wiki/Fishtest-Mathematics.html)). Report W/D/L, TD-diff mean ± paired-bootstrap 95% CI, pentanomial counts.

**B. Offense/defense decomposition per drive** (fixes the confounded `tds`): offensive conversion = TDs per receiving drive; defensive stop rate = 1 − TDs conceded per opponent receiving drive — measured vs gauntlet opponents. Selfplay `tds` demoted to a training diagnostic.

**C. Exploiter probe — the headline exploitability number** ([Timbers et al. 2022](https://arxiv.org/abs/2004.09677); [Gleave et al. 2020](https://arxiv.org/abs/1905.10615); [Wang et al. 2023](https://arxiv.org/abs/2211.00241)). Fixed-budget PPO exploiter (standardize 500M steps, same arch) vs the frozen final checkpoint: exploiter win rate, TDs/game scored on the victim, victim TDs under adversarial pressure, steps-to-first-TD. Cheap variant: offense-only exploiter (receives every drive) isolates "can anyone score on this defense?".

**D. Per-drive micro-stats** (extend the `Log` struct): pickup-attempt rate/success; **fraction of drives where the ball is never picked up** (the avoidance signature — the single most important behavioral metric); mean max ball progress per drive; carrier-advance fraction; passes/handoffs; blocks/turn; knockdowns/cas; turnovers/turn; fouls; possession fraction; stalling indicator (carrier in scoring range ≥1 turn without scoring — distinguishes correct stalling from avoidance). Precedent: sports-analytics fingerprints in [humanoid football, Sci. Robotics 2022](https://arxiv.org/abs/2105.12196).

**E. Human-anchored behavioral panel** (quantifies BC erosion as a curve, not a post-hoc surprise): permanently hold out ~2K of the 24K FUMBBL pairs; report per-head masked CE, top-1/top-3 accuracy, and JS divergence of action-type marginals vs human per game phase, at every eval checkpoint ([AlphaStar Unplugged 2023](https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.03526); [Ke et al.](https://arxiv.org/abs/1905.12888)).

**F. Cross-table + game-theoretic rankings** (once draws break): round-robin over {arm checkpoints ∪ gauntlet}; win-rate heatmap as primary artifact; maxent Nash averaging ([Balduzzi et al., NeurIPS 2018](https://arxiv.org/abs/1806.02643)); α-Rank via OpenSpiel ([Omidshafiei et al. 2019](https://arxiv.org/abs/1903.01373)); RPP for arm-vs-arm population comparisons ([Balduzzi et al., ICML 2019](https://arxiv.org/abs/1901.08106)); non-transitive triad count. **Emit a `DEGENERATE: draw-saturated` flag at draw rate > 95%** — all win/loss-based rankings (Nash, α-Rank, Elo, RPP) are degenerate there; the suite must lead with margin/per-drive/exploiter/behavioral metrics.

**G. Reporting discipline.** No scalar Elo as a primary metric; all stat claims with paired-bootstrap CIs; optional SPRT for promote/reject; pool-Elo retained only for bank-swap gating, ideally augmented with a gauntlet TD-diff gate.

**Build order:** (1) A+B — immediate comparability with existing infra; (2) E — data already exists; (3) D — small C change in `Log`; (4) C — costs GPU but is the only honest exploitability number; (5) F — meaningful only after draws break.
